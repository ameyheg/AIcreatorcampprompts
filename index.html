How to Write a Great Prompt
1

Role

Tell AI who it should be.
"Act like a fun story writer" or "Be my English teacher"

2

Context

Explain your situation.
"I am a 12-year-old from Goa writing a school essay"

3

Task

Say clearly what you want.
"Write a 120-word paragraph about my favorite hobby"

4

Example

Share a reference if you have one.
"Like a fun magazine article"

5

Format

Specify how you want the output.
"Short bullets" or "A story with dialogues"

6

Tone

Mention the tone you want.
"Funny and exciting" or "Simple and clear"

👑 Context is King!

The more details you give, the better the answer! Prompting is like a conversation — keep improving your prompt to get better results.

Safety, Accuracy & Best Practices

✅ Best Practices

  • Don't share personal info: Never enter passwords or photos you don't want to go public.
  • Check before you trust: AI can be wrong sometimes — always double check!
  • Use your brain: AI helps you write, learn and create — but YOU decide what is right.
  • Ask yourself: "Would I be okay if this was seen by everyone?" If NO → Don't share!

🚫 What NOT to Share

  • 🔴 Personal info (phone number, school details, passwords)
  • 🔴 Private chats (WhatsApp, messages)
  • 🔴 Confidential documents or important files
  • 🔴 Photos you don't want to become public

✅ What is Safe to Share

  • 🟢 Made-up (fictional) examples
  • 🟢 Your own notes
  • 🟢 School questions and projects
  • 🟢 General topics (sports, stories, science)

⚠️ AI Mistakes (Hallucinations)

What does it mean? Sometimes AI gives an answer that sounds correct… but is actually wrong or made up.
Why does it happen? The question is not clear, AI doesn't have enough information, or it tries to guess instead of saying "I don't know".

  • Use Thinking mode for step-by-step reasoning
  • Double check answers using textbooks, teachers, or trusted websites
  • Don't blindly copy — if you're not sure, don't use it!
  • You may learn something wrong or use incorrect info in school work — always verify!
